## Artifact Type

## Source Lineage

## Core Idea
Governance becomes most valuable when it stops being interpretive overhead and becomes executable structure. In mature systems, policy is not a binder, memo, or meeting ritual. It becomes encoded operating logic that converts intent into repeatable performance.

## Claim
Governance should behave like a compiler:

1. It receives strategic intent.
2. It translates intent into rules, constraints, and workflows.
3. It detects inconsistency before execution.
4. It prevents drift through repeatable enforcement.
5. It improves system performance by reducing interpretation latency.

## Why This Matters
Organizations often treat governance as a drag on velocity. This artifact reframes governance as a performance system. When governance is well designed, it reduces friction, shortens decision cycles, and increases trust in execution.

## Reusable Framework
Intent → Policy → Workflow → Validation → Performance → Feedback → Governance Refinement.
