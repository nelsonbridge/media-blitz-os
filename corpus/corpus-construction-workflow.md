# Corpus Construction Workflow

## Purpose

This workflow defines how source material becomes the canonical corpus of the Nelson Knowledge System.

The corpus does not pre-exist. It is manufactured from source material through extraction, classification, refinement, relationship mapping, and publication readiness assessment.

## Source Material

Source material includes:

- Chat conversations.
- Uploaded files.
- Google Drive documents.
- GitHub documents.
- Whitepapers.
- Resume and role materials.
- Email patterns.
- Diagrams and visual models.
- External research.
- Personal observations.

Source material is raw input. It is not automatically part of the corpus.

## Workflow Stages

### 1. Capture

Identify source material that may contain reusable intellectual value.

Output: source record.

### 2. Extract

Separate durable ideas from their original context.

Output: candidate artifacts.

### 3. Deduplicate

Compare candidate artifacts against existing corpus items.

Output: new artifact, merged artifact, or derivative relationship.

### 4. Classify

Assign pillar, subpillar, audience, artifact type, and strategic value.

Output: classified artifact.

### 5. Evidence Assessment

Determine whether the artifact is observation, inference, hypothesis, documented claim, or externally verifiable claim.

Output: evidence posture.

### 6. Relationship Mapping

Connect the artifact to source records, pillars, related artifacts, derivative opportunities, and publication pathways.

Output: graph-ready artifact.

### 7. Refinement

Clarify title, summary, core idea, and derivative potential.

Output: refined artifact.

### 8. Canonicalization

Promote the artifact to canonical status when it is stable enough to drive publication or future reasoning.

Output: canonical artifact.

### 9. Publication Packaging

Convert the artifact into one or more public or private assets.

Output: publication packages and derivatives.

### 10. Review and Evolution

Schedule review or revise when evidence, context, or strategic value changes.

Output: updated artifact, supersession, or retirement.

## Artifact Promotion Criteria

A candidate artifact may become canonical when it has:

- Stable title.
- Clear core idea.
- Source lineage.
- Pillar classification.
- Audience classification.
- Evidence posture.
- Relationship map.
- Strategic value.
- Derivative potential.

## Rejection Criteria

Do not promote material to the corpus when it is:

- Casual conversation without durable value.
- Pure venting or transient reaction.
- Too context-specific to reuse.
- Duplicative without adding nuance.
- Unsupported but framed as fact.
- Strategically misaligned.

## Processing Rule

Every execution cycle should process at least one source item or create at least one corpus-supporting artifact unless blocked by access or user-owned decisions.

## Output Locations

- `corpus/artifacts/` for canonical or candidate artifact records.
- `corpus/sources/` for source material records.
- `corpus/relationships/` for relationship maps.
- `corpus/reviews/` for review logs.
- `publishing/packages/` for publication-ready outputs.

## No Idle Application

If source material is not available, create or refine the manufacturing layer. If manufacturing layer is complete, process available conversation-derived knowledge into candidate artifacts.
