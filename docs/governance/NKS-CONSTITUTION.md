# NKS Constitution

Status: Approved  
Version: 1.0  
Authority: Canonical governance

## Purpose

The Knowledge Manufacturing Operating System exists to preserve, validate, compound, and responsibly apply professional and organizational knowledge.

The system shall remain portable, modular, auditable, evidence-aware, licensing-aware, and independent of any single person, model, vendor, brand, storage provider, or publication channel.

## Constitutional Principles

1. **Repository over memory.** Canonical repository state outranks conversation, recollection, generated views, and transient documentation.
2. **Proof before publication.** Claims must carry an explicit evidence posture before release.
3. **Governance before automation.** Automated execution must remain inside defined policy and approval boundaries.
4. **Capabilities over products.** Products and editions are licensed compositions of durable capabilities.
5. **Roles over individuals.** Canonical roles are capabilities, never people, vendors, or models.
6. **Adapters over dependencies.** External systems are replaceable integration surfaces, not architectural boundaries.
7. **Human authority over irreversible action.** Public release, destructive changes, and equivalent preference-bearing actions require explicit authority.
8. **Degraded mode over blocked mode.** Adapter failure queues affected work and does not halt unrelated canonical execution.
9. **Generated views are projections.** Human-readable views may be regenerated and must not silently override canonical records.
10. **Continuity by design.** No contributor, including the founder or any AI implementation, may become a singleton dependency.

## Constitutional Roles

### ANU — Origin Authority

ANU represents foundational intent, strategic direction, constitutional authority, and final governance authority.

ANU is a role. The current founder occupies the role, but the role is not identical to its current holder.

### ENKI — Knowledge Engine

ENKI represents knowledge synthesis, systems reasoning, architecture, implementation guidance, repository intelligence, and technical continuity.

ENKI is a collective capability. No single AI, model, vendor, or human is ENKI. Current and future implementations may contribute to ENKI through governed stewardship.

## Immutable Separation

- Brands describe experiences.
- Products assemble capabilities.
- Licenses enable capabilities.
- Implementations realize capabilities.
- Code references canonical identifiers.

A commercial rename must not require domain refactoring.

## Amendment Rule

Constitutional amendments require:

1. an explicit change record;
2. impact analysis against portability, authority, licensing, and continuity;
3. approval by the current ANU authority;
4. corresponding updates to affected standards and machine-readable registries.

## Governing Maxim

> Capabilities are durable. Implementations are replaceable.
