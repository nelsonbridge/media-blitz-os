# Narrative Arc Standard

## Purpose

The Narrative Arc Standard defines how public-facing corpus artifacts should be shaped for reader progression.

The corpus may store knowledge structurally, but publication must move the reader through recognition, reframe, framework, proof, application, consequence, and invitation.

## Core Arc

```text
Recognition
  ↓
Reframe
  ↓
Framework
  ↓
Proof
  ↓
Application
  ↓
Consequence
  ↓
Invitation
```

## Arc Elements

### 1. Recognition

The reader must recognize the problem before accepting the argument.

This section should answer:

- What does the reader already experience?
- What friction, failure, confusion, or pattern will feel familiar?
- Why does the issue matter now?

### 2. Reframe

The piece must challenge the default interpretation.

This section should answer:

- What is the common but insufficient frame?
- What is the better frame?
- What changes when the reader accepts the reframe?

### 3. Framework

The piece must provide a reusable structure.

This section should answer:

- What model explains the reframe?
- What are the components or sequence?
- How can the reader remember and reuse it?

### 4. Proof

The piece must establish why the reader should trust the claim.

Proof may be:

- Experiential.
- Document-derived.
- Repository-derived.
- Publicly verified.
- Quantitative.

Proof strength must match claim strength.

### 5. Application

The piece must show how the idea can be used.

This section should answer:

- What would a leader, team, or system do differently?
- What questions should the reader ask?
- What operating behavior changes?

### 6. Consequence

The piece must show what changes if the framework is ignored or adopted.

This section should answer:

- What happens if the old frame persists?
- What improves if the new frame is adopted?
- What strategic cost or leverage is at stake?

### 7. Invitation

The piece must end by inviting reflection, application, or dialogue without engagement bait.

This section should answer:

- What should the reader reconsider?
- What should they inspect in their own system?
- What next question naturally follows?

## Publication Gate

A public-facing artifact is not publication-ready until it has been reviewed against this arc.

## Relationship to Source → Proof → Publication

The Narrative Arc Standard does not replace proof. It comes after proof posture is established.

Correct sequence:

```text
Source
  ↓
Proof
  ↓
Narrative Arc
  ↓
Editorial Review
  ↓
Publication
```

## Derivative Rule

Derivatives may compress the arc, but they must preserve its logic.

Example:

- LinkedIn may use Recognition → Reframe → Invitation.
- X thread may use Recognition → Framework → Consequence.
- Instagram carousel may use Recognition → Framework → Application.
- Pinterest may use Framework → Application.

## Failure Modes

Do not publish pieces that are only:

- Abstract claims without recognition.
- Opinions without proof posture.
- Frameworks without application.
- Critiques without consequence.
- Invitations without substance.

## Operating Principle

The corpus stores knowledge. The narrative arc moves people through it.
