# NKS-BRF-000003 — Executive Briefing

## Title

AI Oversight Governance

## Purpose

Explain why AI systems that participate in operational workflows require explicit oversight, accountability, review, and evidence boundaries.

## Executive Thesis

AI governance is not only a policy concern.

It is an operating architecture concern.

When AI systems influence decisions or workflows, leaders need clear boundaries for review, accountability, traceability, and escalation.

## Core Distinction

| Basic Automation | AI-Assisted Operations |
|---|---|
| Runs fixed steps | May influence sequencing or recommendations |
| Simple audit trail | Requires richer review records |
| Narrow exception paths | Requires escalation design |
| Output-focused | Decision context matters |
| Static governance | Requires ongoing review |

## Oversight Model

```text
AI-Assisted Workflow
  ↓
Defined Use Boundary
  ↓
Review Record
  ↓
Human Accountability
  ↓
Escalation Path
  ↓
Continuous Improvement
```

## Required Governance Questions

1. What is the AI system allowed to influence?
2. What remains a human decision?
3. Who is accountable for outcomes?
4. What evidence is retained?
5. What requires review before use?
6. What triggers escalation?
7. How are errors captured and corrected?
8. How is sensitive context protected?
9. How are changes evaluated over time?
10. What conditions make the workflow unsafe to continue?

## Leadership Risks

| Governance Gap | Risk |
|---|---|
| Unclear use boundary | Overreliance or misuse |
| No accountable owner | Diffused responsibility |
| Weak review records | Poor traceability |
| Undefined escalation | Late intervention |
| Unclear evidence boundary | Unsupported claims or decisions |
