## Purpose
Separate executive outcome claims into usable, bounded, and restricted categories before any public-facing release.

## Claim Boundary Principle
Outcome claims may create credibility only when their proof posture is explicit. Unverified numbers can weaken the system by converting evidence into exposure. This packet protects trajectory by separating narrative usefulness from proof readiness.

## Claim Classes

### Class A — Public-Ready with Evidence
Claims supported by independent documentation, public records, signed references, formal case studies, or verifiable artifacts.

Public use: allowed.

### Class B — Bounded Executive Narrative
Claims known internally or narratively useful but not independently packaged.

Public use: allowed only with bounded language, such as "in prior enterprise work," "in one transformation context," or "in an internal operating example."

### Class C — Private / Interview-Only
Claims appropriate for controlled conversations, interviews, diligence discussions, or reference calls.

Public use: not allowed until proof package is complete.

### Class D — Restricted Until Verified
Claims with high specificity, strong financial impact, or reputational sensitivity.

Public use: prohibited until corroborated.

## Safer Public Substitutions
When proof is not yet packaged, use:

- measurable operating gains,
- compressed cycle times,
- accelerated transformation timelines,
- stabilized migrations,
- increased throughput,
- material revenue and margin impact,
- improved executive confidence.

## Required Proof Packet Components
1. Claim statement.
2. Source context.
3. Baseline condition.
4. Measurement method.
5. Time period.
6. Attribution logic.
7. Supporting document or reference.
8. Public-use disposition.
