# Quantitative Verification — NKS-PUB-000005

## Publication

NKS-PUB-000005 — Clarity Is an Economic Instrument

## Verification Question

Is there quantitative support for the bounded claim that organizational role ambiguity is associated with lower performance?

## Evidence Reviewed

### Tubré and Collins (2000)

**Jackson and Schuler (1985) Revisited: A Meta-Analysis of the Relationships Between Role Ambiguity, Role Conflict, and Job Performance**

- Journal: Journal of Management
- Authors: Travis C. Tubré and J. M. Collins
- Finding: Meta-analysis found a negative relationship between role ambiguity and job performance, with moderation by job type and rating source.
- Reported mean relationship: approximately `r = -0.21`.
- Canonical source: https://consensus.app/papers/jackson-and-schuler-1985-revisited-a-metaanalysis-of-the-tubr-collins/4b501456b64e5ea48525c7432cc82120/

### Gilboa et al. (2008)

**A Meta-Analysis of Work Demand Stressors and Job Performance: Examining Main and Moderating Effects**

- Journal: Personnel Psychology
- Authors: S. Gilboa, A. Shirom, Yitzhak Fried, and C. Cooper
- Scope: 169 samples and 35,265 employees.
- Finding: All seven studied work stressors were negatively associated with job performance; role ambiguity and situational constraints had the strongest negative relationships among the included stressors.
- Canonical source: https://consensus.app/papers/a-metaanalysis-of-work-demand-stressors-and-job-gilboa-shirom/0eeb29c7cccf51acb97892d8c8d3ddf5/

### Abramis (1994)

**Work Role Ambiguity, Job Satisfaction, and Job Performance: Meta-Analyses and Review**

- Journal: Psychological Reports
- Scope: 88 studies reviewed; 39 included in meta-analysis.
- Finding: Role ambiguity was significantly and negatively related to satisfaction and performance, but the performance relationship was weak and varied across studies.
- Canonical source: https://consensus.app/papers/work-role-ambiguity-job-satisfaction-and-job-performance-abramis/7baae2584eaf517bb7d1d00d4819f400/

## Supported Publication Boundary

The evidence supports a narrower version of the article's thesis:

> Role ambiguity is associated with lower job performance and therefore represents a plausible source of organizational friction and economic cost.

## Limits

The reviewed evidence does **not** establish:

- a universal causal relationship between clarity and financial performance;
- a specific percentage reduction in delay, rework, escalation, or decision latency;
- a quantified return on investment from a clarity intervention;
- that clarity alone is sufficient to improve organizational outcomes.

The article should retain architectural language such as `can reduce`, `creates avoidable work`, and `operating leverage`, while avoiding unsupported monetary values or universal causal claims.

## Verification Result

PASS WITH BOUNDARIES.

The proof gate may advance to `ready` provided the article preserves the limitations above and cites the verified meta-analytic evidence before public release.
