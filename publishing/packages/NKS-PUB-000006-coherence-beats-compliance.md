## Working Title

Coherence Beats Compliance

## Subtitle

Organizations do not transform because they follow a process. They transform when the operating system becomes coherent.

## Primary Audience

Executives, transformation leaders, agile leaders, operating partners, and technical leaders.

## Strategic Purpose

Challenge performative process adoption and frame transformation around systemic coherence.

## Core Argument

Compliance can create the appearance of alignment while leaving incentives, decisions, language, and execution fragmented. Coherence creates actual operating alignment.

## Draft Outline

1. Compliance can mimic alignment.
2. Coherence aligns decisions, incentives, language, and execution.
3. Transformation fails when process replaces sensemaking.
4. Coherence creates trust and movement.
5. Leaders should design coherence, not merely enforce compliance.

## Key Lines

- Compliance can be visible while coherence is absent.
- Process cannot substitute for sensemaking.
- Coherence is what makes organizations move as systems.
