Self-Healing Systems Need Governance

## Subtitle

A system that can repair itself must also know when not to repair itself.

## Primary Audience

CIOs, CTOs, SRE leaders, platform leaders, operations executives, and security leaders.

## Strategic Purpose

Connect resiliency automation to governance, accountability, and enterprise risk management.

## Core Argument

Self-healing systems can reduce operational burden, but remediation boundaries, rollback logic, escalation, observability, and accountability must be designed explicitly.

## Draft Outline

1. Self-healing changes operational responsibility.
2. Repair without boundaries creates new risks.
3. Rollback and escalation must be designed.
4. Observability is required for trust.
5. Governance makes self-healing useful at enterprise scale.

## Key Lines

- A system that can repair itself must know its limits.
- Resiliency automation still needs accountability.
- Self-healing without governance is just automated uncertainty.
