## Subtitle

The more autonomous the system becomes, the more explicit its governance must be.

## Primary Audience

## Strategic Purpose

Anchor the AI governance pillar by connecting operational autonomy to executive accountability.

## Core Argument

Agentic AI should not be treated as uncontrolled autonomy. It requires escalation paths, authority boundaries, observability, auditability, and human oversight points.

## Draft Outline

1. Operational autonomy changes the risk model.
2. Oversight must be designed, not assumed.
3. Escalation paths and authority boundaries matter.
4. Telemetry and auditability create trust.
5. Governance architecture enables responsible autonomy.

## Key Lines

- Autonomy without oversight is not maturity.
- Governance is the architecture that makes autonomy usable.
- The more an AI system can do, the clearer its limits must be.
