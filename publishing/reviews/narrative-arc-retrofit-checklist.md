# Narrative Arc Retrofit Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist is used to retrofit existing internal drafts so they follow the required reader progression before public release.

## Required Arc

```text
Recognition → Reframe → Framework → Proof → Application → Consequence → Invitation
```

## Retrofit Steps

### 1. Recognition

- [ ] Does the opening name a reader-recognizable problem?
- [ ] Does the reader understand why this issue matters?
- [ ] Does the draft avoid starting too abstractly?

### 2. Reframe

- [ ] Does the draft identify the common but insufficient frame?
- [ ] Does it introduce a sharper frame?
- [ ] Does the reframe feel earned rather than asserted?

### 3. Framework

- [ ] Does the draft provide a reusable model, sequence, or operating principle?
- [ ] Is the framework easy to remember?
- [ ] Can derivatives preserve the framework clearly?

### 4. Proof

- [ ] Is source lineage clear?
- [ ] Is proof category clear?
- [ ] Does claim strength match proof strength?
- [ ] Are required citations or internal evidence references identified?

### 5. Application

- [ ] Does the draft show what the reader can do with the idea?
- [ ] Are practical questions or actions included?
- [ ] Is the application specific enough to be useful?

### 6. Consequence

- [ ] Does the draft show what happens if the old frame persists?
- [ ] Does the draft show what improves if the new frame is adopted?
- [ ] Is the strategic cost or leverage clear?

### 7. Invitation

- [ ] Does the ending invite reflection or application?
- [ ] Does it avoid engagement bait?
- [ ] Does it create a natural next question?

## Completion Standard

A draft may move to `Arc Review Needed` when all sections are present and no section is materially weak.

A draft may move to `Arc Approved` only after editorial review.

## Notes

This checklist applies before publication approval and after proof posture is established.
