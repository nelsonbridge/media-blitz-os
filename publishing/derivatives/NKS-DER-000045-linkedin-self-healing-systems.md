## Core Post Draft

A system that can repair itself must also know when not to repair itself.

Self-healing systems can reduce incident duration and operational burden.

But remediation is still a decision.

Restarting, isolating, routing, rolling back, scaling, or disabling something can affect users, cost, data, and risk.

That means self-healing needs governance:

- allowed actions,
- approval thresholds,
- escalation paths,
- rollback logic,
- observability,
- and accountability.

Self-healing is not just a resiliency feature.

It is an authority model.
