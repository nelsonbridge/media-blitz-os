## Core Post Draft

Ambiguity is expensive.

It shows up as delay, rework, duplicated effort, decision latency, and organizational translation cost.

That is why clarity is not merely a communication preference.

In complex organizations, clarity is an economic instrument.

Clear priorities reduce debate about what matters.
Clear ownership reduces debate about who decides.
Clear constraints reduce debate about what is allowed.
Clear operating principles reduce debate about how to act under pressure.

The clearer the system, the less energy it wastes translating itself.
