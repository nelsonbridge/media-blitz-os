## Core Post Draft

A folder full of documents is not a knowledge system.

A chat history is not a corpus.
An archive is not a strategy.
A search box is not architecture.

Those are source materials.

The corpus begins when durable ideas are extracted, classified, assigned lineage, connected to adjacent ideas, and made reusable.

That distinction matters because many organizations think they have a knowledge problem when they actually have a manufacturing problem.

They are storing information.
They are not converting it into operational knowledge.

The real asset is not the archive.

The real asset is the process that turns source material into reusable intellectual infrastructure.
