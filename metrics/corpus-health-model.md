# Corpus Health Model

## Purpose

Measure whether the Nelson Knowledge System corpus is becoming more coherent, reusable, proof-bounded, and strategically useful.

Corpus health is not the same as corpus size.

## Core Principle

A smaller coherent corpus is more valuable than a larger archive.

## Health Dimensions

| Dimension | Question | Score Range |
|---|---|---:|
| Source Lineage Coverage | Do canonical artifacts trace back to source? | 0–5 |
| Proof Boundary Coverage | Are claim boundaries explicit? | 0–5 |
| Relationship Density | Are artifacts meaningfully connected? | 0–5 |
| Graph Coverage | Are records represented in graph nodes and edges? | 0–5 |
| Opportunity Coverage | Are downstream uses identified beyond publication? | 0–5 |
| Reuse Multiplication | Can one artifact support multiple outputs? | 0–5 |
| Visual Representation | Are complex ideas visually represented when useful? | 0–5 |
| Validation Readiness | Can records be checked against templates/rules? | 0–5 |
| Orphan Control | Are unlinked artifacts identified and resolved? | 0–5 |
| Trajectory Alignment | Does growth support capability strategy? | 0–5 |

Maximum score: 50.

## Health Bands

| Score | Health State | Meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| 0–15 | Archive Drift | Stored material exceeds manufactured knowledge. |
| 16–25 | Managed Repository | Some structure exists, but reuse is inconsistent. |
| 26–35 | Functional Corpus | Artifacts are usable and mostly governed. |
| 36–45 | Knowledge Manufacturing Corpus | Corpus compounds through relationships and reuse. |
| 46–50 | Adaptive Corpus | Corpus can guide its own improvement. |
