# Enki 1.0-rc1 Support Boundaries

## Supported by current evidence

- Product-neutral governed knowledge contracts and canonical mutation boundaries.
- TEST approval, transaction, recovery, forensic, portability, reconciliation, disclosure, transition, and policy workflows.
- Human-state compatibility with stricter consent, privacy, correction, retraction, revocation, and ownership controls.
- Privacy-preserving TEST observability and retention lifecycle behavior.
- Governed synthetic performance characterization and explicit TEST resource budgets.
- Repository-local logical and local physical-separation boundary proofs.
- TEST concurrency, contention, partition-shaped failure, and exact recovery semantics.
- Stable v1.0 consumer API/CLI contract and product-neutral downstream consumption boundaries.
- No-effect TEST integration proofs for Media Blitz, Career Intelligence and Placement, and Personal Cognitive Continuity.

## Not supported or not validated for production

- Production cloud IAM or identity federation.
- Managed production database row-level isolation.
- Production network segmentation.
- Per-tenant production cryptographic key management.
- Production secrets management.
- Independent penetration certification or multitenancy accreditation.
- Production SLA, SLO, latency, throughput, durability, backup, disaster-recovery, or provider availability guarantees.
- Production third-party publishing, career-placement, notification, model-transport, or other external effects unless separately integrated and governed.

## Governance boundary

Downstream products may consume governed Enki state through explicit contracts. They do not define Enki core authority and cannot directly convert predictions, recommendations, publication decisions, inferred preferences, or model outputs into canonical Enki truth.

Any capability outside this document requires separate implementation, validation, authority evidence, and human decision.
