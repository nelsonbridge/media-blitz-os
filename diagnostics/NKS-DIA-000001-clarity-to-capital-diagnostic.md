# NKS-DIA-000001 — Clarity-to-Capital Diagnostic

## Diagnostic Type
Executive operating diagnostic.

## Source Lineage
- NKS-SRC-000005 — The Architecture of Inevitable Value / Enhanced Edition.
- NKS-ART-000019 — The Clarity-to-Capital Cycle.
- NKS-BRF-000002 — Clarity as Economic Instrument.

## Purpose
Assess whether an organization converts clarity into measurable execution value or loses value through ambiguity, duplicated interpretation, delayed decisions, and low-trust coordination.

## Core Thesis
Clarity becomes economically meaningful when it reduces decision latency, coordination cost, rework, escalation load, and risk premiums.

## Scoring Model
Each dimension is scored 1–5.

1 = unclear / unmanaged.
2 = partially visible.
3 = functional but inconsistent.
4 = repeatable and measurable.
5 = self-reinforcing and compounding.

Maximum score: 50.

## Dimensions

### 1. Signal Isolation
Can the organization identify the few truths that matter, or does decision-making drown in noise?

### 2. Shared Truth
Do teams operate from common facts, or does every function maintain its own narrative?

### 3. Decision Latency
How quickly can authority, evidence, and action converge?

### 4. Coordination Cost
How much effort is spent aligning people before work can begin?

### 5. Trust Velocity
Do verified wins compound confidence, or does each initiative restart the trust cycle?

### 6. Escalation Quality
Are escalations used to resolve exceptions, or do they compensate for unclear ownership?

### 7. Rework Load
How much work is repeated because expectations, facts, or decisions were unclear?

### 8. Capital Allocation Confidence
Can leadership allocate money, time, and authority with confidence in the operating signal?

### 9. Feedback Retention
Does the system preserve lessons, or does every cycle rediscover the same failures?

### 10. Compounding Effect
Does each improvement reduce future friction, or does it remain isolated?

## Interpretation

| Score | Interpretation |
|---:|---|
| 0–15 | Ambiguity Tax Dominant |

## Output
The diagnostic should produce:

- Primary ambiguity sources.
- Highest-friction decision points.
- Most valuable clarity intervention.
- Measurable operating indicators.
- Candidate executive story.
- Candidate transformation thesis.

## Public-Use Boundary
This diagnostic may be used as a framework. Specific financial claims require evidence packets and claim-level proof review.
