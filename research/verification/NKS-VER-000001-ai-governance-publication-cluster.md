# NKS-VER-000001 — AI Governance Publication Cluster Verification

## Purpose

Verification note for NKS-PUB-000009 through NKS-PUB-000012 before expanding into full public-facing drafts.

## Publications Covered

- NKS-PUB-000009 — Agentic AI Requires Human Oversight Architecture
- NKS-PUB-000010 — Security Automation Needs Guardrails
- NKS-PUB-000011 — Agentic DevOps Beyond CI/CD
- NKS-PUB-000012 — Self-Healing Systems Need Governance

## Verification Sources Consulted

### NIST AI Risk Management Framework

NIST AI RMF supports framing AI risk management around trustworthy AI, risk management practices, and incorporating trustworthiness considerations into the design, development, use, and evaluation of AI systems.

Useful for:

- Oversight architecture
- Risk posture
- Trustworthiness framing
- Critical infrastructure AI risk practices

### OWASP Top 10 for Large Language Model Applications / GenAI Security Project

OWASP identifies security and safety risks for generative AI, including risks associated with prompt injection, insecure output handling, excessive agency, overreliance, and related LLM application security concerns.

Useful for:

- Guardrails
- Excessive agency framing
- Security boundaries
- Overreliance concerns

### Google SRE Automation Material

Google SRE material supports the broader reliability framing that automation should improve systems reliability and reduce operational burden while remaining governed by engineering practice.

Useful for:

- Automation and reliability framing
- Self-healing / remediation governance
- Operational discipline

## Verification Result

The four AI governance publication packages are suitable for internal full-draft expansion if claims remain framed as architecture, governance, and operational principles.

Public publication still requires final editorial review and careful citation insertion.

## Claim Boundaries

Allowed in drafts:

- Agentic or automated systems require explicit governance boundaries.
- AI security systems should account for risks such as excessive agency and overreliance.
- Automation should be designed with reliability, observability, and accountable operation in mind.
- AI risk management should be integrated into design, development, use, and evaluation.

Avoid without further verification:

- Vendor-specific performance claims.
- Claims that a specific tool or framework is universally sufficient.
- Claims about legal compliance unless jurisdiction and date are verified.
- Claims about current market adoption rates.
